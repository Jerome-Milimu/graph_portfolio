Personal Refelection:

With Pearson’s correlation coefficient of (r = -0.052) between Male Life Expectancy and Employment Rate for 2019, it is observed that, countries where a larger share of men are employed are not necessarily the ones where men live longer, underscoring that longevity is shaped more by health systems, living conditions and broader socio-economic factors than by labour-market participation alone
